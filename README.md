### About

No lab, no institution, no degree — self-taught, working from open datasets and reproducible code. What I actually do: take a hypothesis, try to break it with real data before anyone else does, and report whatever's left standing — including the parts that don't hold up.

I care more about a method being honest than a result being exciting. Null results go in the README, not the trash. If you find an error in anything here, that's useful to me, not an attack.

Currently interested in: behavioral subtyping in ADHD, open reanalysis of public clinical datasets, and the difference between what a statistical test shows and what people want it to show.

### Featured work

**[the-Allosteric-Sprint-hypothesis](https://github.com/d1d2dopamine/the-Allosteric-Sprint-hypothesis)** — tested whether ADHD splits into behavioral subtypes on attention-task data, across two independent open datasets. One part of the hypothesis held up under correction and permutation testing; another part didn't and is reported as such. Full stats, methodology, known limitations, and a complete log of every correction made along the way are in the repo — not summarized twice here.

`Python` · `pandas` · `NumPy` · `SciPy` · `statsmodels` · `matplotlib`
